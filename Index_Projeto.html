    2024 Summer Olympics

    The 2024 Summer Olympics, officially called the Games of the XXXIII Olympiad (in French: *Jeux de la XXXIIIe Olympiade*) and commonly known as Paris 2024, was an international multi-sport event held from July 26 to August 11, 2024, in France.

    Paris was the main host city, with 16 other cities spread across Metropolitan France, as well as a sub-host site in Tahiti — an island within the French overseas territory and the overseas collectivity of French Polynesia.

    Having previously hosted the Games in 1900 and 1924, Paris became the second city, after London, to host the Summer Olympics three times. These Games also marked the centenary of Paris 1924.

    The 2024 Games feature the debut of breakdancing as an Olympic event and were the last Olympic Games held under the IOC presidency of Thomas Bach. The Paris Olympics cost approximately 9 billion euros.

    2024 Summer Paralympics

    The XVII Summer Paralympic Games, also known as the Paris 2024 Paralympic Games, was a multi-sport event for athletes with disabilities, organized by the International Paralympic Committee (IPC) and hosted in Paris, France.

    The competition took place from August 28 to September 8, 2024, and marked the first equality between men and women across all sports, except for a few disciplines.

    It was the first time Paris hosted the Paralympic Games. This edition was notable for achieving the highest average attendance, with 12.1 million tickets sold, earning it the nickname of the Paralympic Revolution.
